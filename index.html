    TSAVO NATIONAL PARK

    The park forms the largest protected area in Kenya and is home to most of the larger mammals, vast herds of dust –red elephant, Rhino, buffalo, lion, leopard, pods of hippo, crocodile, waterbucks, lesser Kudu, gerenuk and the prolific bird life
    features 500 recorded species.



    QUEEN ELIZABETH NATIONAL PARK

    This park brings together the most diverse ecosystem in Africa.The park also harbors primates including chimpanzees,Waterbuck, giant forest hog, topi, hyena, and crocodiles.Open savannah dotted with acacia and euphorbia trees provide habitat for, leopard, elephant, lion, Uganda kob, in addition to big herds of buffalo.



    KIDEPO VALLEY NATIONAL PARK

    Kidepo Valley National Park Is approximately 1,445 sq.Kilometers in size and it is located in the Karamoja region in the northeast of Uganda.The park has a semi-arid climate with one rainy season per year between April and September and rainfall is light.



    BWINDI IMPENETRATBLE NATIONAL PARK

    Its mist-covered hillsides are blanketed by one of Uganda's oldest and most biologically diverse rainforests. More famously, this “impenetrable forest” also protects an estimated 400 mountain gorillas – roughly half of the world’s population, including several habituated groups, which can be tracked.



    MGAHINGA GORILLA NATIONAL PARK

    Though it is very important as it is one of the two parks in Uganda that are habitat for the endangered mountain gorillas. This park adjoins Rwanda’s Volcano National Park, and the Democratic Republic of Congo’s Virunga National Park.

    LAKE MBURO NATIONAL PARK

    The attractive acacia-dotted savanna (158 square miles) is home to huge herds of impala, as well as uncommon topi, eland, klipspringer and other antelope. Other animals found in the park include Zebras and buffaloes.



    THE NILE

    located close to Jinja at its outflow from Lake Victoria. This is the longest river in Africa stretching through Sudan, Egypt and finally to the Mediterranean Sea. The source of the river was discovered by John Speke in 1862.



    LAKE BUNYONYI

    ”Place of many little birds” lies in south western Uganda in Kabale district. The lake is the deepest in Uganda and is the second deepest lake in Africa. It is a magical spot that has been renamed the ”Switzerland of Africa” and as its name ”Bunyonyi” suggests in the local language.

    The property boasts an outdoor swimming pool with a view of the Lake Victoria. Guests can relax at the garden or the shared lounge and have a drink at the bar.
